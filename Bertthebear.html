Bert the Bear

Made of a Kevlar felt and Epoxy resin composite, the same materials used in types of bulletproof armor, Bert the Bear isn't your average teddy bear; He's a statement about gun violence.

A teddy bear is typically something you would associate with children, unfortunately, mass shootings have also come to be associated with children. This as sad as it may has become fact, you hear about a school shooting or massacre at a birthday party and think, "Thank god it happened somewhere else, what a shame." Then you forget about it once the shooting leaves the media cycle. While this comment and forget behavior may not apply to those who lost loved ones, were injured, or are parents themselves, for a majority of Americans this phenomenon is true. The tragedy of a shooting that doesn't direct impact you, is fleeting; it is just something you hear about in the news. There is no tangibility to it.

Bert the Bear is meant to give tangibility to this issue by serving as a physical manifestation of the issues surrounding children in mass shootings. The use of Epoxied Kevlar, which is typically in types of bullet proof armor, was used to make the teddy bear. Combining an item synonymous with children, with materials used for war is supposed to make you uncomfortable. It should prompt you to consider the environment in which our children now live. Bert is a physical object you can hold, he is not an article or abstract idea that leaves your mind, but a tangible representation of the mass shooting reality we so often ignore.
